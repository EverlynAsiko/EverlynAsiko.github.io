Expertise: Python Programming, Neural Machine Translation, Data Science

Hi there! My name is Everlyn Asiko Chimoto. I am currently a PhD student at the University of Cape Town, supervised by Prof. Bruce Bassett. My research focuses on developing data and model-efficient techniques in machine translation for low-resource languages, including data pruning and active learning. I am also interested in LLMs for low-resource settings.

Previously, I completed MSc in Mathematical Sciences from the African Institute for Mathematical Sciences (AIMS) / University of Cape Town (UCT) with my research still focusing on Machine translation for low-resource languages and a BSc in Computer Science from Masinde Muliro University of Science and Technology. I also enjoy teaching and have previously been a technical mentor and tutor for Moringa School and NeuroMatch summer school 2021. I am also a member of Masakhane where I have helped organize several workshop: AfricaNLP, NLP@Indaba.
